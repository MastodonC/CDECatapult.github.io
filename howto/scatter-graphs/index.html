Scatter graphs

Scatter graphs (or scatter plots) allow us to show the relationship between two related values, in a similar way to a line graph.

They are useful to visually display a relationship between these values, and can demonstrate (or disprove) a trend.

That's better!

Here is the same chart with some visual improvements. And here's what they are:

  • Remove Chartjunk - chart borders, grid lines and unnecessary tick mark have gone, leaving the chart feeling less cluttered.
  • Use softer lines for the axes - strong black can look too harsh on white, by softening it a touch the axes look more considered and also allow your data to stand out.
  • Make sure your scale is appropriate. - using D3 means that your scale will probably be set sensibly for your dataset, but use your common-sense. There is no point in having loads of axis with no data.

Other ideas and suggestions

Here's are some more points to note when dealing with scatter plots:

  • Don't mix numerical and non-numerical information. For example, plotting distance away from work (numerical) against perceived happiness will be difficult to make use of. Better to plot distance as 'near' and 'far' in this case.
  • You can add a third dimension to a scatter plot to make it a bubble chart. In this example, the third dimension is the size of the circle at each point. (Note that this uses the nvd3 library)
